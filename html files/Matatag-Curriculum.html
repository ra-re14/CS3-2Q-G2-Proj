The MATATAG Curriculum is an initiative by the Department of Education (DepEd) in the Philippines, aimed at enhancing the teaching and learning experience. This revamped curriculum spans from kindergarten to 10th grade and is focused on developing well-rounded, job-ready citizens..

MATATAG will have four critical components:

  • MA:Make the curriculum relevant to produce competent and job-ready, active, and responsible citizens.
  • TA:Take steps to accelerate delivery of basic education facilities and services.
  • TA:Take good care of learners by promoting learner well-being, inclusive education, and a positive learning environment.
  • G:Give support to teachers to teach better.

Positive Effects of Matatag Curriculum:

  • Promotes:Promotes comprehensive education with practical skills and resource management.
  • Integrates:Integrates science, technology, and other life aspects.
  • Good Care:Take good care of learners by promoting learner well-being, inclusive education, and a positive learning environment.
  • Understanding:Uses spiral progression to deepen students' understanding over time.

Negative Effects of Matatag Curriculum:

  • Concerns:Concerns over inclusive education and equity.
  • Shortages:Shortages of resources and infrastructure.
  • Inadequate:Inadequate government policies.

The MATATAG Curriculum seeks to reduce the number of competencies and prioritize the development of foundational skills—such as literacy, numeracy, and socio-emotional skills—among Kindergarten to Grade 3 learners. This approach aims to streamline and simplify the current K to 12 curriculum..
